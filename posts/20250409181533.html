words-fifth-day

释义列表

  1. comedy - n.

    • paraphrase：funny play or film
    • synonym：humor, farce
    • example：The comedy was so amusing that the audience laughed even when the actors forgot their lines.

  2. consumption - n.

    • paraphrase：act of using up resources
    • synonym：use, expenditure
    • example：The government urged people to reduce energy consumption because power plants were overloaded.

  3. defeat - v./n.

    • paraphrase：win against someone; failure
    • synonym：beat, overcome
    • example：If we defeat this team, we’ll advance to the finals, which is our ultimate goal.

  4. confess - v.

    • paraphrase：admit wrongdoing
    • synonym：admit, acknowledge
    • example：He confessed to stealing the money, but his family refused to believe it.

  5. enterprise - n.

    • paraphrase：business project or company
    • synonym：company, venture
    • example：The new enterprise aims to provide jobs in areas where unemployment is high.

  6. locality - n.

    • paraphrase：specific area or neighborhood
    • synonym：region, district
    • example：The new park is located in a quiet locality where residents often walk their dogs.

  7. fare - n./v.

    • paraphrase：① (n.) money paid for travel; ② (v.) perform
    • synonym：① ticket price; ② manage
    • example：Bus fares increased last week, which made daily commuting more expensive.

  8. comprise - v.

    • paraphrase：consist of
    • synonym：include, contain
    • example：The committee comprises experts from various fields who meet monthly.

  9. alter - v.

    • paraphrase：change slightly
    • synonym：modify, adjust
    • example：She had to alter her dress because it was too long to wear to the party.

  10. clue - n.

    • paraphrase：hint to solve a problem
    • synonym：hint, sign
    • example：The detective found a clue under the table, which led him to the suspect.

  11. endorse - v.

    • paraphrase：publicly support
    • synonym：approve, support
    • example：The celebrity endorsed the product, which boosted its sales immediately.

  12. alternation - n.

    • paraphrase：repeated change between states
    • synonym：shift, switch
    • example：The alternation between hot and cold weather confused the plants’ growth cycles.

  13. relief - n.

    • paraphrase：feeling of comfort after stress
    • synonym：ease, comfort
    • example：She sighed with relief when she heard her son was safe after the accident.

  14. mentor - n./v.

    • paraphrase：experienced advisor
    • synonym：guide, teacher
    • example：Her mentor taught her negotiation skills, which helped her secure the deal.

  15. economical - adj.

    • paraphrase：saving money or resources
    • synonym：thrifty, efficient
    • example：The car is economical on fuel, which makes it ideal for long trips.

  16. savage - adj./n.

    • paraphrase：extremely violent or cruel
    • synonym：brutal, fierce
    • example：The savage storm destroyed houses that had stood for decades.

  17. airline - n.

    • paraphrase：company providing air transport
    • synonym：carrier, aviation
    • example：The airline canceled flights due to fog, which stranded hundreds of passengers.

  18. toss - v.

    • paraphrase：throw lightly
    • synonym：throw, fling
    • example：He tossed the keys onto the table before collapsing onto the sofa.

  19. soak - v.

    • paraphrase：make completely wet
    • synonym：drench, saturate
    • example：If you soak the beans overnight, they’ll cook faster the next day.

  20. tropical - adj.

    • paraphrase：related to hot, humid regions
    • synonym：equatorial, lush
    • example：Tropical fruits like mangoes grow abundantly in areas near the equator.

  21. sector - n.

    • paraphrase：section of an economy or area
    • synonym：division, zone
    • example：The technology sector is booming, which attracts many investors.

  22. negligible - adj.

    • paraphrase：too small to matter
    • synonym：insignificant, minor
    • example：The error in the report was negligible, so no one bothered to correct it.

  23. disgrace - n./v.

    • paraphrase：loss of respect; shame
    • synonym：shame, dishonor
    • example：The scandal brought disgrace to his family, who had always been respected.

  24. amend - v.

    • paraphrase：make small improvements
    • synonym：revise, correct
    • example：The law was amended to protect workers’ rights after protests erupted.

  25. favor - n./v.

    • paraphrase：kind act; prefer
    • synonym：kindness, support
    • example：She asked me for a favor because she couldn’t finish the task alone.

  26. conceive - v.

    • paraphrase：form an idea or become pregnant
    • synonym：imagine, devise
    • example：He conceived the plan while sitting in the park where he often relaxed.

  27. victim - n.

    • paraphrase：person harmed by an action
    • synonym：casualty, sufferer
    • example：The victim of the scam lost all his savings, which left him devastated.

  28. gross - adj.

    • paraphrase：total before deductions; disgusting
    • synonym：total, vulgar
    • example：The gross income of the company rose, but profits fell due to high costs.

  29. sovereign - n./adj.

    • paraphrase：supreme ruler; independent
    • synonym：monarch, supreme
    • example：The sovereign power of the nation lies with its people, not a single ruler.

  30. render - v.

    • paraphrase：cause to be; provide
    • synonym：make, give
    • example：The storm rendered the roads impassable, which delayed rescue teams.

  31. premium - n./adj.

    • paraphrase：high-quality; extra payment
    • synonym：superior, extra
    • example：Customers pay a premium for organic products because they’re healthier.

  32. evade - v.

    • paraphrase：escape or avoid cleverly
    • synonym：avoid, dodge
    • example：The politician evaded the question when asked about corruption.

  33. illustrate - v.

    • paraphrase：explain with examples or images
    • synonym：demonstrate, show
    • example：The teacher used diagrams to illustrate how the human heart works.

  34. restrict - v.

    • paraphrase：limit movement or action
    • synonym：limit, confine
    • example：The law restricts smoking in public places to protect non-smokers.

  35. stereotype - n./v.

    • paraphrase：fixed oversimplified idea
    • synonym：cliché, generalize
    • example：The film broke the stereotype that heroes must always be physically strong.

  36. conservation - n.

    • paraphrase：protection of natural resources
    • synonym：preservation, protection
    • example：Conservation efforts saved the forest where endangered species live.

  37. contract - n./v.

    • paraphrase：legal agreement; shrink
    • synonym：agreement, shrink
    • example：She signed a contract that requires her to work for the company for two years.

  38. suspicious - adj.

    • paraphrase：doubtful or distrustful
    • synonym：doubtful, wary
    • example：The suspicious package was reported to the police, who evacuated the area.

  39. expertise - n.

    • paraphrase：specialized knowledge
    • synonym：skill, knowledge
    • example：His expertise in engineering helped solve the bridge’s design flaw.

  40. ethnic - adj.

    • paraphrase：related to cultural groups
    • synonym：cultural, racial
    • example：The festival celebrates ethnic traditions that have been passed down for generations.

  41. vessel - n.

    • paraphrase：ship or container; blood tube
    • synonym：ship, container
    • example：The vessel carried medical supplies to the island where hospitals were understaffed.

  42. safeguard - v./n.

    • paraphrase：protect from harm
    • synonym：protect, defense
    • example：Regular backups safeguard data in case the computer system crashes.

  43. biography - n.

    • paraphrase：story of a person’s life
    • synonym：life story, memoir
    • example：The biography revealed secrets about the author’s childhood that shocked readers.

  44. abolish - v.

    • paraphrase：officially end a system
    • synonym：eliminate, end
    • example：The government abolished the tax after protests erupted across the country.

  45. pension - n.

    • paraphrase：regular payment after retirement
    • synonym：retirement fund, allowance
    • example：He relies on his pension to cover living costs since he stopped working.

  46. conductor - n.

    • paraphrase：person directing music; material transmitting electricity
    • synonym：director, leader
    • example：The conductor raised his baton, and the orchestra began playing.

  47. firm - adj./n.

    • paraphrase：solid; company
    • synonym：hard, company
    • example：The mattress was too firm, so she couldn’t sleep comfortably.

  48. purse - n./v.

    • paraphrase：small bag for money; tighten lips
    • synonym：handbag, clutch
    • example：She lost her purse while shopping, which contained her ID and credit cards.

  49. attach - v.

    • paraphrase：fasten or join
    • synonym：fix, connect
    • example：Attach the file to the email before sending it to the manager.

  50. irony - n.

    • paraphrase：opposite of what’s expected
    • synonym：sarcasm, paradox
    • example：The irony is that the fire station burned down while firefighters were on vacation.
