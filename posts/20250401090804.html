释义列表

  1. significant - adj.

    • paraphrase ：very important or noticeable
    • synonym ：important, major
    • example ：The discovery was significant because it solved a problem scientists had studied for years.

  2. craft - n.

    • paraphrase ：skill in making things by hand
    • synonym ：art, skill
    • example ：She used her craft to create pottery, which she sold at the local market every weekend.

  3. delay - v.

    • paraphrase ：make something happen later
    • synonym ：postpone, stall
    • example ：The flight was delayed due to bad weather, so we waited at the airport until midnight.

  4. valley - n.

    • paraphrase ：low area between hills or mountains
    • synonym ：canyon, basin
    • example ：The river flowed through the valley, and people built villages along its banks.

  5. principle - n.

    • paraphrase ：basic rule or belief
    • synonym ：rule, standard
    • example ：She refused to lie, as honesty was a principle she valued deeply.

  6. architect - n.

    • paraphrase ：person who designs buildings
    • synonym ：designer, planner
    • example ：The architect drew plans for the bridge, which would connect two cities.

  7. complex - adj.

    • paraphrase ：difficult to understand; having many parts
    • synonym ：complicated, intricate
    • example ：The instructions were too complex, so he asked his teacher for help.

  8. garment - n.

    • paraphrase ：piece of clothing
    • synonym ：clothing, attire
    • example ：She bought a new garment for the party, but it didn’t fit well.

  9. revolve - v.

    • paraphrase ：move in a circle around something
    • synonym ：rotate, circle
    • example ：The Earth revolves around the Sun, which takes one year to complete.

  10. decorate - v.

    • paraphrase ：make something look attractive
    • synonym ：adorn, beautify
    • example ：They decorated the room with lights, although they forgot to add balloons.

  11. drug - n.

    • paraphrase ：substance used as medicine or for pleasure
    • synonym ：medicine, pill
    • example ：The doctor prescribed a drug to treat her headache, but she had to avoid alcohol.

  12. grip - v.

    • paraphrase ：hold something tightly
    • synonym ：grasp, clutch
    • example ：She gripped the rope tightly while climbing, even though her hands hurt.

  13. affection - n.

    • paraphrase ：warm feeling of love or care
    • synonym ：love, fondness
    • example ：The dog showed affection by wagging its tail whenever its owner came home.

  14. pile - n.

    • paraphrase ：stack of things placed together
    • synonym ：heap, mound
    • example ：There was a pile of dirty dishes in the sink, so she decided to wash them.

  15. elite - adj.

    • paraphrase ：belonging to a superior group
    • synonym ：exclusive, top
    • example ：The elite athletes trained daily, but they still struggled to win the final race.

  16. messenger - n.

    • paraphrase ：person who delivers messages
    • synonym ：courier, carrier
    • example ：The messenger brought a letter, which contained urgent news about the war.

  17. shift - v.

    • paraphrase ：move or change position
    • synonym ：adjust, move
    • example ：He shifted the furniture to clean the floor, but he accidentally broke a vase.

  18. abundant - adj.

    • paraphrase ：existing in large amounts
    • synonym ：plentiful, rich
    • example ：The forest had abundant wildlife, so hikers often saw deer and birds.

  19. overturn - v.

    • paraphrase ：turn over or reverse a decision
    • synonym ：flip, reverse
    • example ：The court overturned the old law, which made many people happy.

  20. servant - n.

    • paraphrase ：person who works in a household
    • synonym ：helper, attendant
    • example ：The servant cooked meals every day, but she also cleaned the entire house.

  21. trend - n.

    • paraphrase ：popular style or direction
    • synonym ：fashion, tendency
    • example ：Wearing oversized jackets became a trend, although some found them uncomfortable.

  22. accompany - v.

    • paraphrase ：go somewhere with someone
    • synonym ：join, escort
    • example ：He accompanied his sister to the hospital because she was too scared to go alone.

  23. eminent - adj.

    • paraphrase ：famous and respected
    • synonym ：famous, distinguished
    • example ：The eminent professor wrote a book, which became popular worldwide.

  24. alike - adj.

    • paraphrase ：similar in appearance or nature
    • synonym ：similar, identical
    • example ：The two paintings looked alike, but one had a hidden signature in the corner.

  25. operation - n.

    • paraphrase ：medical procedure or planned action
    • synonym ：procedure, surgery
    • example ：The operation took three hours, but the patient recovered quickly.

  26. musician - n.

    • paraphrase ：person who plays or writes music
    • synonym ：player, artist
    • example ：The musician practiced the violin daily, although he also loved the piano.

  27. multiple - adj.

    • paraphrase ：many; having several parts
    • synonym ：many, numerous
    • example ：She had multiple assignments to finish, so she stayed up late working.

  28. wander - v.

    • paraphrase ：walk aimlessly
    • synonym ：roam, stroll
    • example ：He liked to wander in the forest, even though his parents warned him about getting lost.

  29. exchange - v.

    • paraphrase ：give and receive something
    • synonym ：trade, swap
    • example ：They exchanged gifts at the party, which made everyone smile.

  30. mixture - n.

    • paraphrase ：combination of different things
    • synonym ：blend, mix
    • example ：The soup was a mixture of vegetables and meat, which tasted delicious.

  31. execute - v.

    • paraphrase ：carry out a plan or order
    • synonym ：perform, achieve
    • example ：The team executed the plan perfectly, although they faced many obstacles.

  32. blank - adj.

    • paraphrase ：empty; without writing
    • synonym ：empty, bare
    • example ：She stared at the blank screen, unsure how to start her presentation.

  33. notion - n.

    • paraphrase ：idea or belief
    • synonym ：idea, concept
    • example ：He had a notion that cats were smarter than dogs, but his friend disagreed.

  34. industrial - adj.

    • paraphrase ：related to factories or production
    • synonym ：manufacturing, factory
    • example ：The industrial area was noisy, so workers wore ear protection.

  35. extension - n.

    • paraphrase ：additional part or time
    • synonym ：addition, expansion
    • example ：She requested an extension for her project because she needed more data.

  36. transfer - v.

    • paraphrase ：move from one place to another
    • synonym ：move, shift
    • example ：He transferred money to his friend’s account, but the transaction failed at first.

  37. cure - n.

    • paraphrase ：solution to a disease
    • synonym ：remedy, treatment
    • example ：Scientists hope to find a cure for the virus, which has affected millions.

  38. discipline - n.

    • paraphrase ：self-control or strict training
    • synonym ：control, training
    • example ：Learning an instrument requires discipline, but it brings great joy.

  39. sponsor - v.

    • paraphrase ：support financially
    • synonym ：fund, support
    • example ：The company sponsored the event, which allowed free entry for everyone.

  40. yell - v.

    • paraphrase ：shout loudly
    • synonym ：shout, scream
    • example ：She yelled for help when she fell, and a neighbor rushed to assist her.

  41. tough - adj.

    • paraphrase ：hard; difficult
    • synonym ：hard, challenging
    • example ：The exam was tough, but she passed by studying every night.

  42. labor - n.

    • paraphrase ：physical or mental work
    • synonym ：work, effort
    • example ：After hours of labor, they finished building the fence around the farm.

  43. genuine - adj.

    • paraphrase ：real; sincere
    • synonym ：real, authentic
    • example ：Her apology was genuine, so he forgave her for the mistake.

  44. passion - n.

    • paraphrase ：strong enthusiasm or love
    • synonym ：enthusiasm, love
    • example ：His passion for cooking led him to open a restaurant, which became successful.

  45. function - n.

    • paraphrase ：purpose or role
    • synonym ：purpose, role
    • example ：The function of this button is to start the machine, but it sometimes jams.

  46. knit - v.

    • paraphrase ：make fabric by looping yarn
    • synonym ：weave, stitch
    • example ：She knit a scarf for her brother, who loved wearing it in winter.

  47. announce - v.

    • paraphrase ：declare publicly
    • synonym ：declare, proclaim
    • example ：The mayor announced a new park, which would open next summer.

  48. seal - v.

    • paraphrase ：close securely
    • synonym ：close, shut
    • example ：He sealed the jar tightly so the food inside wouldn’t spoil.

  49. obtain - v.

    • paraphrase ：get through effort
    • synonym ：get, acquire
    • example ：She obtained a rare stamp, which she added to her collection.

  50. quantity - n.

    • paraphrase ：amount or number
    • synonym ：amount, volume
    • example ：The recipe required a small quantity of salt, but he added too much by accident.
