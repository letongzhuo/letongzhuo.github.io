单词释义

  1. reverse - v./n./adj.

    • paraphrase：to go backward or change to the opposite

    • synonym：undo, invert

    • example：If you accidentally delete a file, you can reverse the action by pressing Ctrl+Z, which is a handy feature unless you’ve already closed the program.

  2. refute - v.

    • paraphrase：to prove something wrong

    • synonym：deny, disprove

    • example：Although the scientist presented strong evidence, her critics tried to refute her claims by pointing out minor flaws in the data.

  3. household - n./adj.

    • paraphrase：a family or group living together; related to home

    • synonym：family, home

    • example：Smart devices like thermostats are now common in many households, whereas a decade ago they were considered luxury items.

  4. overhaul - v./n.

    • paraphrase：to repair or improve completely

    • synonym：renovate, rebuild

    • example：The company plans to overhaul its website, which is outdated, so that users can navigate it more easily.

  5. wit - n.

    • paraphrase：clever humor or intelligence

    • synonym：humor, cleverness

    • example：She answered the tough question with wit, even though she was nervous, which impressed everyone in the room.

  6. holder - n.

    • paraphrase：a person or thing that holds something

    • synonym：owner, keeper

    • example：The holder of the record must defend their title unless someone else breaks their achievement.

  7. counterpart - n.

    • paraphrase：a person or thing that corresponds to another

    • synonym：equivalent, match

    • example：The CEO met with her counterpart from the rival company to discuss cooperation, whereas their teams focused on competition.

  8. spring - n./v.

    • paraphrase：the season after winter; to jump suddenly

    • synonym：leap, bounce

    • example：When flowers bloom in spring, the landscape becomes colorful, which attracts many tourists.

  9. fortunate - adj.

    • paraphrase：lucky or having good fortune

    • synonym：lucky, blessed

    • example：We were fortunate to find a parking spot near the entrance, although the lot was almost full.

  10. sneak - v.

  • paraphrase：to move quietly and secretly

  • synonym：slip, creep

  • example：He tried to sneak into the room without being noticed, but the creaky floor gave him away.

  1. vice - n.

  • paraphrase：a bad habit or moral weakness

  • synonym：sin, fault

  • example：Gambling is a vice that can ruin lives if it isn’t controlled, whereas moderate hobbies like reading are harmless.

  1. landscape - n.

  • paraphrase：the visible features of an area of land

  • synonym：scenery, view

  • example：The landscape changed dramatically after the storm, which uprooted trees and altered the river’s course.

  1. runway - n.

  • paraphrase：a strip where planes take off and land

  • synonym：airstrip, track

  • example：The pilot aborted the landing when he noticed debris on the runway, although the weather conditions were perfect.

  1. beneath - prep./adv.

  • paraphrase：under or below something

  • synonym：under, underneath

  • example：She found her lost earring beneath the couch, where it had rolled after falling off.

  1. mediate - v.

  • paraphrase：to help resolve a dispute

  • synonym：arbitrate, negotiate

  • example：The teacher had to mediate the argument between the students before it escalated into a fight.

  1. indicative - adj.

  • paraphrase：showing or suggesting something

  • synonym：suggestive, revealing

  • example：His nervousness was indicative of his lack of preparation, which became obvious during the presentation.

  1. federal - adj.

  • paraphrase：related to a central government

  • synonym：national, central

  • example：The federal agency announced new regulations, whereas local governments retained control over certain policies.

  1. preceding - adj.

  • paraphrase：coming before in time or order

  • synonym：previous, prior

  • example：The preceding chapter explained the basics, which are essential to understand the advanced topics covered later.

  1. faith - n.

  • paraphrase：strong belief or trust

  • synonym：trust, belief

  • example：She kept her faith in the team even after they lost, although many fans had given up hope.

  1. exposure - n.

  • paraphrase：the state of being exposed to something

  • synonym：contact, experience

  • example：Long exposure to sunlight can damage your skin, which is why wearing sunscreen is important.

  1. cue - n./v.

  • paraphrase：a signal to do something

  • synonym：signal, prompt

  • example：The actor missed his cue, so the scene had to be reshot, which delayed the production.

  1. alliance - n.

  • paraphrase：a union or partnership

  • synonym：partnership, coalition

  • example：The two companies formed an alliance to develop new technology, whereas their competitors worked independently.

  1. cease - v.

  • paraphrase：to stop or end

  • synonym：stop, halt

  • example：The rain finally ceased, although the ground remained wet for hours afterward.

  1. lottery - n.

  • paraphrase：a game of chance with prizes

  • synonym：raffle, draw

  • example：Winning the lottery might seem like a dream, but it can also bring unexpected problems if not managed wisely.

  1. preliminary - adj.

  • paraphrase：coming before the main event

  • synonym：initial, preparatory

  • example：The preliminary results were promising, which encouraged the team to continue their research.

  1. liberty - n.

  • paraphrase：freedom to act or think

  • synonym：freedom, independence

  • example：The new law protects individual liberty, whereas older regulations were more restrictive.

  1. threshold - n.

  • paraphrase：the entrance or starting point

  • synonym：doorway, brink

  • example：She paused at the threshold of the room, unsure whether to enter, although she had been invited.

  1. stability - n.

  • paraphrase：the state of being steady

  • synonym：balance, firmness

  • example：The economy regained stability after the crisis, which reassured investors and businesses.

  1. strike - v./n.

  • paraphrase：to hit or refuse work as protest

  • synonym：hit, protest

  • example：The workers decided to strike unless their demands for better pay were met.

  1. prosecute - v.

  • paraphrase：to take legal action against someone

  • synonym：sue, charge

  • example：The authorities will prosecute anyone who breaks the law, no matter what their position is.

  1. ambiguous - adj.

  • paraphrase：unclear or having multiple meanings

  • synonym：vague, unclear

  • example：His answer was ambiguous, which left everyone confused about what he actually meant.

  1. liable - adj.

  • paraphrase：legally responsible

  • synonym：responsible, accountable

  • example：The company is liable for any damages caused by its products, even if the fault lies with a supplier.

  1. complaint - n.

  • paraphrase：an expression of dissatisfaction

  • synonym：grievance, protest

  • example：She filed a complaint about the noisy neighbors, although they claimed they weren’t making much noise.

  1. paradise - n.

  • paraphrase：a perfect or heavenly place

  • synonym：heaven, utopia

  • example：The island was like paradise, with its white sand and clear water, which made it a popular vacation spot.

  1. outset - n.

  • paraphrase：the beginning of something

  • synonym：start, beginning

  • example：From the outset, it was clear that the project would be challenging, although the team was optimistic.

  1. condense - v.

  • paraphrase：to make shorter or more concentrated

  • synonym：compress, summarize

  • example：The editor asked her to condense the report so that it would fit into two pages, whereas the original was five.

  1. extraction - n.

  • paraphrase：the act of removing something

  • synonym：removal, withdrawal

  • example：The extraction of natural resources can harm the environment if it isn’t done responsibly.

  1. sacred - adj.

  • paraphrase：holy or highly valued

  • synonym：holy, divine

  • example：The temple is considered sacred, which is why visitors must follow strict rules when entering.

  1. demographic - n./adj.

  • paraphrase：related to population statistics

  • synonym：population, statistical

  • example：The demographic data revealed that most customers were young adults, whereas the company had assumed an older audience.

  1. dairy - n./adj.

  • paraphrase：related to milk products

  • synonym：milk, lactose

  • example：She avoids dairy because she’s lactose intolerant, although she loves cheese.

  1. variation - n.

  • paraphrase：a change or difference

  • synonym：difference, fluctuation

  • example：The variation in temperature between day and night is extreme in deserts, which makes survival difficult.

  1. dissolve - v.

  • paraphrase：to mix into a liquid or end officially

  • synonym：melt, disband

  • example：The sugar will dissolve faster in hot water, whereas salt takes longer.

  1. quota - n.

  • paraphrase：a fixed share or limit

  • synonym：limit, allocation

  • example：The factory met its production quota, which earned the workers a bonus.

  1. bleak - adj.

  • paraphrase：dreary or hopeless

  • synonym：gloomy, dismal

  • example：The future looked bleak after the company’s bankruptcy, although some employees remained hopeful.

  1. decent - adj.

  • paraphrase：acceptable or respectable

  • synonym：respectable, adequate

  • example：He offered a decent price for the car, which convinced the seller to accept the deal.

  1. breach - n./v.

  • paraphrase：a break or violation

  • synonym：violation, break

  • example：The hacker’s breach of security exposed sensitive data, which alarmed the company’s clients.

  1. triumph - n./v.

  • paraphrase：a great victory or success

  • synonym：victory, success

  • example：Their triumph in the championship was celebrated by fans, although the opponents were disappointed.

  1. participate - v.

  • paraphrase：to take part in something

  • synonym：join, engage

  • example：She was eager to participate in the debate, even though she was nervous about public speaking.

  1. originate - v.

  • paraphrase：to begin or create

  • synonym：begin, start

  • example：The tradition originated centuries ago, which is why it’s still deeply rooted in the culture.

  1. anonymous - adj.

  • paraphrase：unknown or unnamed

  • synonym：unnamed, unidentified

  • example：The donation was made by an anonymous benefactor, whose identity remains a mystery.
