释义列表

  1. nevertheless - adv.

    • paraphrase：in spite of that
    • synonym：however, still
    • example：The weather app predicted rain; nevertheless, we went hiking because we trusted the sunny patches breaking through the clouds.

  2. humble - adj.

    • paraphrase：not proud, simple
    • synonym：modest, plain
    • example：Although he won the award, his humble speech focused on thanking his team rather than his own achievements.

  3. scope - n.

    • paraphrase：the range of something
    • synonym：extent, range
    • example：The project’s scope expanded unexpectedly when we discovered new data that needed analysis.

  4. heap - n.

    • paraphrase：a large messy pile
    • synonym：pile, stack
    • example：I finally cleaned my room, where clothes had been tossed in a heap near the bed for weeks.

  5. perish - v.

    • paraphrase：die or disappear
    • synonym：die, vanish
    • example：If global temperatures keep rising, many species might perish unless we take immediate action.

  6. likelihood - n.

    • paraphrase：probability
    • synonym：chance, possibility
    • example：There’s a high likelihood the concert will be canceled unless ticket sales improve this week.

  7. forecast - n./v.

    • paraphrase：predict future events
    • synonym：predict, estimate
    • example：Meteorologists forecast heavy snow, which might delay flights unless airports prepare in advance.

  8. astonish - v.

    • paraphrase：surprise greatly
    • synonym：amaze, shock
    • example：The magician’s trick astonished the audience, who gasped when a live dove appeared from his empty hat.

  9. reservation - n.

    • paraphrase：a booked arrangement
    • synonym：booking, appointment
    • example：We made a dinner reservation, but the restaurant canceled it because they closed early for repairs.

  10. inform - v.

    • paraphrase：tell someone facts
    • synonym：notify, tell
    • example：Please inform me when the package arrives so I can sign for it before the delivery person leaves.

  11. intercourse - n.

    • paraphrase：communication or interaction
    • synonym：communication, exchange
    • example：Healthy social intercourse is vital, whereas isolation can harm mental health over time.

  12. concise - adj.

    • paraphrase：clear and brief
    • synonym：succinct, brief
    • example：Her presentation was concise, which impressed the team even though they usually prefer detailed reports.

  13. mutual - adj.

    • paraphrase：shared by both
    • synonym：common, joint
    • example：We reached a mutual agreement after discussing terms that satisfied both parties.

  14. cooperative - adj.

    • paraphrase：willing to work together
    • synonym：collaborative, helpful
    • example：If everyone stays cooperative, we’ll finish the project faster than expected.

  15. instant - adj.

    • paraphrase：immediate
    • synonym：immediate, quick
    • example：The app provides instant translations, which is helpful when traveling to countries where language barriers exist.

  16. mild - adj.

    • paraphrase：not strong or harsh
    • synonym：gentle, moderate
    • example：The doctor recommended a mild exercise routine unless the patient experiences pain.

  17. indulge - v.

    • paraphrase：allow oneself to enjoy
    • synonym：pamper, spoil
    • example：I’ll indulge in dessert tonight even though I’m trying to eat healthier this month.

  18. institute - n./v.

    • paraphrase：organization or establish
    • synonym：organization, establish
    • example：The university instituted new rules after students requested changes to the grading system.

  19. burden - n.

    • paraphrase：heavy load or responsibility
    • synonym：load, responsibility
    • example：Carrying heavy backpacks daily can become a burden unless students use lockers provided by the school.

  20. chop - v.

    • paraphrase：cut into pieces
    • synonym：cut, slice
    • example：She chopped vegetables while listening to a podcast, which made the tedious task enjoyable.

  21. detach - v.

    • paraphrase：remove or separate
    • synonym：remove, disconnect
    • example：The magnet allows you to detach the keychain easily when you need to use just one key.

  22. hoist - v.

    • paraphrase：lift with effort
    • synonym：lift, raise
    • example：Workers hoisted the flag once the ceremony began, even though the wind made it challenging.

  23. redeem - v.

    • paraphrase：exchange for value
    • synonym：recover, reclaim
    • example：You can redeem your coupon at checkout if you meet the minimum purchase requirement.

  24. prevalent - adj.

    • paraphrase：common in a place
    • synonym：common, widespread
    • example：Online shopping is prevalent nowadays, whereas physical stores face more competition.

  25. medieval - adj.

    • paraphrase：related to the Middle Ages
    • synonym：ancient, old
    • example：The castle’s medieval architecture, which includes stone walls and narrow windows, attracts history lovers.

  26. tide - n.

    • paraphrase：rise and fall of the sea
    • synonym：current, wave
    • example：Beachgoers should check the tide schedule before swimming because strong currents can be dangerous.

  27. procession - n.

    • paraphrase：a line of moving people/vehicles
    • synonym：parade, march
    • example：The wedding procession walked slowly down the aisle while guests stood to take photos.

  28. preferable - adj.

    • paraphrase：better than others
    • synonym：better, favored
    • example：Public transport is preferable to driving in cities where parking is expensive and limited.

  29. subordinate - adj./n.

    • paraphrase：lower in rank
    • synonym：junior, assistant
    • example：Managers should listen to subordinate staff members whose ideas might improve workflows.

  30. empirical - adj.

    • paraphrase：based on observation
    • synonym：observed, factual
    • example：Scientists rely on empirical evidence, which must be verified through experiments.

  31. propel - v.

    • paraphrase：push forward
    • synonym：push, drive
    • example：The new engine design propels the car faster while using less fuel than older models.

  32. accessory - n.

    • paraphrase：extra item for style/function
    • synonym：addition, attachment
    • example：She bought a phone accessory that holds earphones, which prevents them from tangling.

  33. string - n.

    • paraphrase：thin cord or series
    • synonym：cord, sequence
    • example：He tied the package with string because tape wasn’t available when he needed to seal it.

  34. aspect - n.

    • paraphrase：a part or feature
    • synonym：feature, element
    • example：One aspect of remote work I enjoy is flexibility, although isolation can be challenging.

  35. regime - n.

    • paraphrase：government system
    • synonym：government, administration
    • example：The new regime introduced policies that affected businesses unless they complied with regulations.

  36. monarch - n.

    • paraphrase：king or queen
    • synonym：ruler, sovereign
    • example：The monarch announced a holiday, which citizens celebrated with parades and festivals.

  37. correlate - v.

    • paraphrase：have a connection
    • synonym：link, associate
    • example：Studies show that sleep quality correlates with productivity, although individual habits vary.

  38. rescue - v.

    • paraphrase：save from danger
    • synonym：save, recover
    • example：Firefighters rescued the cat stuck in a tree after neighbors reported hearing meows for hours.

  39. intrinsic - adj.

    • paraphrase：natural and essential
    • synonym：inherent, natural
    • example：Curiosity is an intrinsic human trait that drives exploration even when risks exist.

  40. installment - n.

    • paraphrase：a part of a series
    • synonym：episode, payment
    • example：I paid the second installment for the course, which grants access to advanced modules next month.

  41. compound - n./v.

    • paraphrase：mix or combination
    • synonym：mixture, combine
    • example：Stress can compound health issues if left unmanaged, whereas relaxation techniques help.

  42. compose - v.

    • paraphrase：create or write
    • synonym：create, write
    • example：She composed an email while waiting for her flight, which departed later than scheduled.

  43. pave - v.

    • paraphrase：cover with hard surface
    • synonym：cover, surface
    • example：The city paved the road after residents complained about potholes that damaged their cars.

  44. incline - v./n.

    • paraphrase：slope or tend toward
    • synonym：slope, lean
    • example：I’m inclined to accept the job offer unless the salary doesn’t meet my expectations.

  45. initiate - v.

    • paraphrase：start something
    • synonym：start, begin
    • example：The company will initiate training sessions next week, which all employees must attend.

  46. resultant - adj.

    • paraphrase：occurring as a result
    • synonym：resulting, consequent
    • example：The resultant traffic jam delayed commuters for hours after the accident blocked two lanes.

  47. tumble - v.

    • paraphrase：fall suddenly
    • synonym：fall, drop
    • example：The toddler tumbled off the chair but laughed instead of crying when her mom caught her.

  48. tempt - v.

    • paraphrase：attract or persuade
    • synonym：entice, lure
    • example：The aroma of fresh bread tempted customers to enter the bakery even if they weren’t hungry.

  49. lawsuit - n.

    • paraphrase：legal dispute in court
    • synonym：case, litigation
    • example：The lawsuit was settled out of court after both parties agreed to compromise.

  50. bias - n.

    • paraphrase：preference or prejudice
    • synonym：prejudice, favoritism
    • example：Journalists should avoid bias when reporting news so that audiences can form their own opinions.

确定化与最小化
确定化 用画表法 第一步：第一列写出所有的状态，第一行写出所有的弧名 状态应该不是所有节点的排列组合，要不先把所有单个节点的状态写出来，然后单个节点推出来新的状态之后再把这个新状态写到第一列 第二步：给左边第一列的所有状态编号，0、1、2、……，然后把表中的所有状态用编号代替 第三步：根据这个编号得出的新表画出确定化的图 什么是终态终态：原图上的终态，和包含所有节点的状态 举个例子 最小化 记住一句话，分组后的元素，经过a弧到达的状态在同一组中，经过b弧到达的状态也在同一组中（这两个组不一定相同） 根据上一个得出的确定化图写 第一步：分组，把非终态分成一组，把终态分成一组 对两个组分别用相同的方法进行处理，所以光以非终态组为例说明 第二步：写出每一个元素通过a弧的状态 第三步：看是否在同一组中，是的话直接看通过b弧的状态，不是的话，把不同的状态按相同的组进行分类 第四步：看分了之后的组通过b弧的状态，这些状态在同一个组中的话，那么这些状态就可以合并

理解积分
理解三重积分的方式 一重积分可以用面积理解，二重积分可以用体积理解，三重积分显然无法用这种方法理解，我如何理解三重积分 物理量的总和：当被积函数表示密度（如质量密度、电荷密度）时，三重积分的结果是该物理量在三维区域内的总量。例如，物体的总质量可通过积分密度函数得到。 概率与统计：若被积函数是概率密度函数，三重积分可计算三维区域内事件发生的概率。 几何测度：当被积函数恒为1时，三重积分的结果即为区域的体积，此时与几何直观一致。 数学抽象：作为多维空间中的累积操作，三重积分可视为在三个独立方向上的加权求和，无需依赖四维几何直观，而是通过逐次积分或坐标变换进行计算。 物理定律应用：如高斯定理中，三重积分用于计算场源（如电荷总量），与曲面积分结合揭示物理规律。 使用质量密度理解三重积分 假设有一个三维物体，其质量密度随位置变化，我们可以用三重积分求出它的总质量。这个例子将帮助你建立对三重积分的直观理解。 例子：计算一个长方体的总质量 问题设定： 假设有一个长方体物体，占据空间区域： xxx 方向范围：0≤x≤20 \leq x \leq...
