单词释义

  1. mature - adj./v.

    • paraphrase：fully grown or developed

    • synonym：grown, ripe, adult

    • example：Although the fruit looks ripe, it needs more time to mature before we can eat it.

  2. imminent - adj.

    • paraphrase：about to happen very soon

    • synonym：approaching, nearing

    • example：The storm clouds suggest rain is imminent, so we should hurry home unless we want to get wet.

  3. convention - n.

    • paraphrase：a formal meeting or accepted way of doing things

    • synonym：conference, custom

    • example：At the tech convention, which lasted three days, experts discussed AI trends whereas beginners attended workshops.

  4. portrait - n.

    • paraphrase：a painting or photo of a person

    • synonym：picture, image

    • example：She painted a portrait that captured his smile, which reminded her of their childhood together.

  5. suppress - v.

    • paraphrase：to stop by force or control

    • synonym：restrain, quell

    • example：The government tried to suppress the protest, but it grew larger when news spread online.

  6. excess - n./adj.

    • paraphrase：more than what is needed

    • synonym：surplus, extra

    • example：If you eat in excess, you might gain weight unless you exercise regularly.

  7. religious - adj.

    • paraphrase：related to religion or very careful

    • synonym：devout, faithful

    • example：She follows her routine with religious dedication, whereas her brother prefers spontaneity.

  8. sanction - n./v.

    • paraphrase：official permission or punishment

    • synonym：approval, penalty

    • example：The UN imposed sanctions, which angered the country’s leaders although they claimed innocence.

  9. field - n./v.

    • paraphrase：an area of land or a subject of study

    • synonym：domain, meadow

    • example：He works in the medical field, where research is advancing rapidly because of new technology.

  10. acclaim - n./v.

    • paraphrase：public praise

    • synonym：praise, applause

    • example：The film received critical acclaim when it premiered, even though its budget was small.

  11. sow - v.

    • paraphrase：to plant seeds

    • synonym：plant, scatter

    • example：Farmers sow crops in spring so that they can harvest them before winter arrives.

  12. exotic - adj.

    • paraphrase：unusual and from a distant place

    • synonym：foreign, unusual

    • example：She loves exotic fruits, whereas her brother prefers local apples and oranges.

  13. legitimate - adj.

    • paraphrase：legal or reasonable

    • synonym：valid, lawful

    • example：Although his request seemed odd, it was legitimate because he followed the rules.

  14. likewise - adv.

    • paraphrase：in the same way

    • synonym：similarly, also

    • example：She enjoys hiking; likewise, her brother loves outdoor activities.

  15. suspicion - n.

    • paraphrase：a feeling that something is wrong

    • synonym：doubt, mistrust

    • example：He had a suspicion that the deal was too good, which turned out to be true.

  16. agitate - v.

    • paraphrase：to make someone worried or to stir

    • synonym：disturb, upset

    • example：The loud noise agitated the dog, so it barked until the sound stopped.

  17. tease - v.

    • paraphrase：to make fun of or annoy playfully

    • synonym：mock, joke

    • example：She teased her brother about his haircut, but she stopped when he got upset.

  18. sufficient - adj.

    • paraphrase：enough for a purpose

    • synonym：adequate, enough

    • example：Unless you have sufficient evidence, the court won’t accept your claim.

  19. overlap - v./n.

    • paraphrase：to partly cover or share features

    • synonym：intersect, coincide

    • example：Their work schedules overlap, which makes it hard to meet for lunch.

  20. swell - v./n.

    • paraphrase：to become larger or a rise

    • synonym：expand, increase

    • example：Her ankle began to swell after she twisted it, so she applied ice.

  21. steer - v.

    • paraphrase：to guide or control direction

    • synonym：direct, navigate

    • example：He steered the conversation away from politics because it caused arguments.

  22. controversy - n.

    • paraphrase：public disagreement

    • synonym：dispute, debate

    • example：The new law sparked controversy, whereas supporters argued it was necessary.

  23. ruling - n.

    • paraphrase：an official decision

    • synonym：verdict, judgment

    • example：The court’s ruling was fair, although some people still disagreed.

  24. tow - v./n.

    • paraphrase：to pull with a rope or chain

    • synonym：drag, haul

    • example：The truck towed the broken car to the garage, where mechanics could fix it.

  25. pride - n.

    • paraphrase：a feeling of satisfaction

    • synonym：dignity, self-respect

    • example：She felt pride when she finished the project, which took months of hard work.

  26. brace - v./n.

    • paraphrase：to prepare for something difficult or a support

    • synonym：strengthen, support

    • example：Brace yourself for bad news, although I hope it’s not too serious.

  27. obligation - n.

    • paraphrase：something you must do

    • synonym：duty, responsibility

    • example：He felt an obligation to help his neighbor, even though he was busy.

  28. navy - n.

    • paraphrase：the part of the military that operates at sea

    • synonym：fleet, maritime force

    • example：The navy protects the coast, whereas the army defends land borders.

  29. virtue - n.

    • paraphrase：good moral quality

    • synonym：goodness, morality

    • example：Patience is a virtue that helps people stay calm when things go wrong.

  30. mere - adj.

    • paraphrase：nothing more than

    • synonym：only, just

    • example：The idea seemed mere gossip at first, but it turned out to be true.

  31. casual - adj.

    • paraphrase：not formal or relaxed

    • synonym：informal, relaxed

    • example：He wore casual clothes to the party, whereas others dressed formally.

  32. gum - n.

    • paraphrase：a sticky substance or part of the mouth

    • synonym：adhesive, chewing gum

    • example：She blew a bubble with her gum, which popped and stuck to her face.

  33. stem - n./v.

    • paraphrase：the main part of a plant or to stop something

    • synonym：stalk, originate

    • example：The problem stems from poor communication, so we need to talk more.

  34. status - n.

    • paraphrase：social or professional position

    • synonym：rank, standing

    • example：His status at work improved after he completed the project successfully.

  35. contingent - adj./n.

    • paraphrase：dependent on something else or a group

    • synonym：dependent, delegation

    • example：Our plans are contingent on the weather, so we’ll decide tomorrow.

  36. robust - adj.

    • paraphrase：strong and healthy

    • synonym：sturdy, vigorous

    • example：The robust design of the bridge ensures it can withstand heavy storms.

  37. supervise - v.

    • paraphrase：to watch and direct

    • synonym：oversee, manage

    • example：She supervises the team, which includes five new employees.

  38. absorb - v.

    • paraphrase：to take in or soak up

    • synonym：soak, assimilate

    • example：The sponge absorbed the water quickly, whereas the cloth took longer.

  39. regardless - adv.

    • paraphrase：without considering

    • synonym：anyway, nevertheless

    • example：Regardless of the cost, they decided to travel because they needed a break.

  40. obscure - adj./v.

    • paraphrase：not well known or to hide

    • synonym：unclear, hidden

    • example：The artist was obscure at first, but her work gained fame over time.

  41. screen - n./v.

    • paraphrase：a flat surface or to examine

    • synonym：monitor, filter

    • example：The doctor screened patients for symptoms, although most were healthy.

  42. apart - adv.

    • paraphrase：separated by distance

    • synonym：separate, away

    • example：They live apart now, whereas they used to share an apartment.

  43. brand - n./v.

    • paraphrase：a type of product or to mark

    • synonym：label, trademark

    • example：She prefers this brand of shoes because they last longer than others.

  44. assemble - v.

    • paraphrase：to gather or put together

    • synonym：gather, build

    • example：They assembled the furniture, which took hours because the instructions were unclear.

  45. pool - n./v.

    • paraphrase：a small area of water or to combine

    • synonym：pond, combine

    • example：We pooled our money to buy a gift, although some couldn’t contribute much.

  46. disturb - v.

    • paraphrase：to interrupt or bother

    • synonym：interrupt, annoy

    • example：Please don’t disturb him while he’s working unless it’s urgent.

  47. elaborate - adj./v.

    • paraphrase：detailed or to explain more

    • synonym：complex, detailed

    • example：She gave an elaborate explanation, which confused some listeners.

  48. susceptible - adj.

    • paraphrase：easily affected

    • synonym：vulnerable, prone

    • example：Children are susceptible to colds, so they should wash their hands often.

  49. halve - v.

    • paraphrase：to divide into two equal parts

    • synonym：split, divide

    • example：If we halve the recipe, we’ll have enough for four people instead of eight.

  50. march - v./n.

    • paraphrase：to walk in a group or a month

    • synonym：parade, walk

    • example：They marched through the city, where crowds cheered them on.
