释义列表

  1. index - n.

    • paraphrase：a list of topics or names in a book, often at the end
    • synonym：list, guide
    • example：She checked the index of the textbook to find the chapter about climate change, but it wasn’t listed there.

  2. occupation - n.

    • paraphrase：a job or activity you do regularly
    • synonym：job, profession
    • example：His occupation as a teacher kept him busy, though he sometimes missed working outdoors.

  3. implication - n.

    • paraphrase：a possible result or meaning of something
    • synonym：consequence, effect
    • example：The implication of his words was clear: if we failed the project, the team would lose funding.

  4. operator - n.

    • paraphrase：a person who controls equipment or a system
    • synonym：controller, technician
    • example：The operator fixed the machine, which had stopped working during the night shift.

  5. inevitable - adj.

    • paraphrase：certain to happen; unavoidable
    • synonym：unavoidable, certain
    • example：The rain made the delay inevitable, so we waited inside the station.

  6. gallery - n.

    • paraphrase：a room or building where art is shown
    • synonym：museum, exhibit
    • example：They visited the gallery to see the new paintings, but the sculpture section was closed.

  7. sue - v.

    • paraphrase：take legal action against someone
    • synonym：prosecute, charge
    • example：She decided to sue the company because they refused to pay for the accident.

  8. fore - n.

    • paraphrase：the front part of something
    • synonym：front, forefront
    • example：The captain stood at the fore of the ship, which gave him a clear view of the storm.

  9. ambition - n.

    • paraphrase：a strong desire to achieve something
    • synonym：goal, dream
    • example：His ambition to become a doctor drove him to study late every night.

  10. structure - n.

    • paraphrase：the way parts are arranged in a system or object
    • synonym：framework, design
    • example：The structure of the essay was clear, but the conclusion needed more work.

  11. verdict - n.

    • paraphrase：a formal decision in a court case
    • synonym：judgment, ruling
    • example：The jury’s verdict was “not guilty,” which surprised many people in the courtroom.

  12. automation - n.

    • paraphrase：using machines to do tasks instead of humans
    • synonym：mechanization, robotics
    • example：Automation reduced factory costs, but it also eliminated many jobs.

  13. harsh - adj.

    • paraphrase：unpleasantly rough or severe
    • synonym：rough, severe
    • example：The harsh winter made travel difficult, so schools closed for a week.

  14. mathematical - adj.

    • paraphrase：related to math or calculations
    • synonym：numerical, arithmetic
    • example：She solved the mathematical problem quickly, though her classmates struggled.

  15. handicap - n.

    • paraphrase：a condition that limits a person’s abilities
    • synonym：disability, limitation
    • example：His handicap made walking difficult, but he used a wheelchair to stay independent.

  16. identification - n.

    • paraphrase：proof of who someone is
    • synonym：ID, documentation
    • example：The officer asked for identification, so she showed her driver’s license.

  17. ingredient - n.

    • paraphrase：a part of a mixture or recipe
    • synonym：component, element
    • example：Flour is the main ingredient in bread, but yeast makes it rise.

  18. party - n.

    • paraphrase：a social event or a group with shared goals
    • synonym：gathering, group
    • example：The birthday party was fun, but it ended early because of the rain.

  19. merit - n.

    • paraphrase：the quality of being good or deserving praise
    • synonym：value, worth
    • example：The proposal had merit, so the committee approved it after discussion.

  20. spokesperson - n.

    • paraphrase：a person who speaks for a group or organization
    • synonym：representative, agent
    • example：The spokesperson announced the new policy, which would take effect next month.

  21. derive - v.

    • paraphrase：get something from a source
    • synonym：obtain, extract
    • example：Many medicines derive from plants, which grow in tropical forests.

  22. algorithm - n.

    • paraphrase：set of steps to solve a problem
    • synonym：method, procedure
    • example：The algorithm sorted the data quickly, but it sometimes made errors.

  23. stance - n.

    • paraphrase：a position or opinion about something
    • synonym：attitude, position
    • example：His stance on environmental issues was clear, though some disagreed.

  24. cherish - v.

    • paraphrase：value or love deeply
    • synonym：treasure, adore
    • example：She cherished the necklace her grandmother gave her, which was over 50 years old.

  25. facilitate - v.

    • paraphrase：make something easier
    • synonym：ease, assist
    • example：The app facilitates online learning, so students can study from home.

  26. frustrate - v.

    • paraphrase：make someone feel annoyed or defeated
    • synonym：annoy, discourage
    • example：The slow progress frustrated him, but he kept trying to finish the task.

  27. legacy - n.

    • paraphrase：something left behind by a person or history
    • synonym：inheritance, heritage
    • example：His legacy as a leader inspired many, though his methods were sometimes criticized.

  28. fatigue - n.

    • paraphrase：extreme tiredness
    • synonym：exhaustion, weariness
    • example：After working 12 hours, she felt fatigue, but she still cooked dinner.

  29. highland - n.

    • paraphrase：mountainous or elevated land
    • synonym：upland, plateau
    • example：The highland region was cold, so farmers grew crops only in summer.

  30. optimum - adj.

    • paraphrase：best or most favorable
    • synonym：ideal, perfect
    • example：The optimum temperature for the experiment was 25°C, which they maintained carefully.

  31. organization - n.

    • paraphrase：a group working together for a purpose
    • synonym：association, institution
    • example：The organization donated food to villages, which helped reduce hunger.

  32. excel - v.

    • paraphrase：be very good at something
    • synonym：shine, succeed
    • example：She excelled in math, though she found languages challenging.

  33. mood - n.

    • paraphrase：how someone feels emotionally
    • synonym：emotion, temper
    • example：His mood improved after he ate, though he was still tired.

  34. impair - v.

    • paraphrase：weaken or damage
    • synonym：weaken, harm
    • example：Loud noise can impair hearing, so workers wore ear protection.

  35. deduce - v.

    • paraphrase：reach a conclusion using logic
    • synonym：conclude, infer
    • example：From the footprints, he deduced that the animal was large, but he couldn’t identify it.

  36. variance - n.

    • paraphrase：difference or inconsistency
    • synonym：difference, variation
    • example：There was a variance in the test results, so the scientists repeated the experiment.

  37. decisive - adj.

    • paraphrase：able to make decisions quickly
    • synonym：resolute, firm
    • example：The manager’s decisive action saved the project, which had been failing.

  38. formulate - v.

    • paraphrase：create or prepare carefully
    • synonym：develop, devise
    • example：They formulated a plan to reduce costs, but it required approval from the board.

  39. cyberspace - n.

    • paraphrase：the internet and digital world
    • synonym：internet, online
    • example：Hackers attacked systems in cyberspace, which caused widespread panic.

  40. suburb - n.

    • paraphrase：area outside a city center
    • synonym：outskirts, neighborhood
    • example：They moved to the suburb for quieter life, though commuting took longer.

  41. sum - n.

    • paraphrase：total amount from adding numbers
    • synonym：total, amount
    • example：The sum of 5 and 7 is 12, which he wrote on the board.

  42. impressive - adj.

    • paraphrase：causing admiration or respect
    • synonym：amazing, striking
    • example：The skyscraper’s height was impressive, but its design was even more unique.

  43. bore - v.

    • paraphrase：make someone feel uninterested
    • synonym：tire, weary
    • example：The long speech bored the audience, so many people left early.

  44. branch - n.

    • paraphrase：part of a tree or a division of an organization
    • synonym：limb, division
    • example：The company opened a new branch in Tokyo, which increased its global reach.

  45. preface - n.

    • paraphrase：introduction to a book or speech
    • synonym：introduction, prologue
    • example：The preface explained the book’s purpose, but the first chapter was more engaging.

  46. equity - n.

    • paraphrase：fairness or ownership in a company
    • synonym：fairness, shares
    • example：The company promoted equity in hiring, which ensured equal opportunities for all.

  47. convene - v.

  • paraphrase：bring people together for a meeting
  • synonym：assemble, gather
  • example：The committee convened to discuss the budget, but they couldn’t reach an agreement.
  1. turbine - n.
  • paraphrase：machine that uses wind, water, or steam to generate power
  • synonym：rotor, engine
  • example：The wind turbine produced energy for the village, which reduced their need for coal.
  1. beam - n./v.
  • paraphrase：① (n.) long thick piece of wood/metal; ② (v.) smile brightly
  • synonym：① bar, plank; ② grin, smile
  • example：The wooden beam supported the roof, and the workers secured it with nails.
  1. liberate - v.
  • paraphrase：set someone or something free
  • synonym：free, release
  • example：The army fought to liberate the city, which had been occupied for five years.
