释义列表

  1. cunning - adj./n.

    • paraphrase：clever at deceiving others
    • synonym：sly, crafty
    • example：Although the fox was cunning, it couldn’t escape the trap that the farmer had set near the fence.

  2. burst - v./n.

    • paraphrase：break open suddenly or explode
    • synonym：explode, rupture
    • example：The balloon burst when it touched the thorny bush, which startled the children playing nearby.

  3. superfluous - adj.

    • paraphrase：more than needed; unnecessary
    • synonym：excess, extra
    • example：The teacher asked us to remove superfluous details from the report so it would be clearer.

  4. qualitative - adj.

    • paraphrase：related to quality rather than quantity
    • synonym：descriptive, subjective
    • example：The study focused on qualitative data, such as personal experiences, while ignoring numbers.

  5. dismiss - v.

    • paraphrase：send away or reject as unimportant
    • synonym：reject, disregard
    • example：The manager dismissed the idea because it was too costly, even though the team supported it.

  6. ceiling - n.

    • paraphrase：upper interior surface of a room
    • synonym：roof, upper limit
    • example：The ceiling of the old house had cracks, which let in cold air during winter storms.

  7. architecture - n.

    • paraphrase：design style of buildings
    • synonym：structure, design
    • example：Gothic architecture uses tall spires, which symbolize a connection to heaven.

  8. relieve - v.

    • paraphrase：reduce pain or stress
    • synonym：ease, alleviate
    • example：The medicine relieved her headache, so she could finish her work before the deadline.

  9. visual - adj.

    • paraphrase：related to sight
    • synonym：visible, optical
    • example：The visual effects in the movie were stunning, although the plot was confusing.

  10. perspective - n.

    • paraphrase：way of viewing something
    • synonym：viewpoint, angle
    • example：From his perspective, the plan was perfect, but others saw flaws in it.

  11. desperate - adj.

    • paraphrase：extremely needing or hopeless
    • synonym：urgent, hopeless
    • example：They were desperate for food after the storm destroyed their crops.

  12. variable - adj./n.

    • paraphrase：likely to change; a changing factor
    • synonym：unstable, factor
    • example：The experiment’s results were variable because the temperature fluctuated daily.

  13. endeavor - n./v.

    • paraphrase：try hard to achieve something
    • synonym：attempt, effort
    • example：She endeavored to finish the project on time, even though the team faced many delays.

  14. jurisdiction - n.

    • paraphrase：legal authority over an area
    • synonym：authority, control
    • example：The crime occurred outside the police’s jurisdiction, so they couldn’t investigate it.

  15. questionnaire - n.

    • paraphrase：set of written questions for research
    • synonym：survey, form
    • example：Participants filled out a questionnaire to share their opinions about the new policy.

  16. underlie - v.

    • paraphrase：be the hidden cause of something
    • synonym：base, support
    • example：Economic inequality underlies many social conflicts that governments struggle to resolve.

  17. prolong - v.

    • paraphrase：make something last longer
    • synonym：extend, lengthen
    • example：The doctor warned that smoking would prolong his recovery from the illness.

  18. presently - adv.

    • paraphrase：soon or currently
    • synonym：shortly, now
    • example：She is presently working on the report, which she will submit by tomorrow morning.

  19. launch - v./n.

    • paraphrase：start or introduce something new
    • synonym：begin, initiate
    • example：The company will launch its new product next month if the final tests are successful.

  20. scrutiny - n.

    • paraphrase：close examination or criticism
    • synonym：inspection, analysis
    • example：The proposal faced intense scrutiny because its costs were unusually high.

  21. urban - adj.

    • paraphrase：related to cities
    • synonym：city, metropolitan
    • example：Urban areas often struggle with traffic congestion, which rural regions rarely experience.

  22. instinct - n.

    • paraphrase：natural behavior or feeling
    • synonym：intuition, impulse
    • example：Her instinct told her to avoid the dark alley, where strange noises echoed at night.

  23. peculiar - adj.

    • paraphrase：strange or unusual
    • synonym：odd, bizarre
    • example：The cat’s peculiar habit of staring at the wall puzzled everyone in the house.

  24. undergo - v.

    • paraphrase：experience something (often difficult)
    • synonym：endure, experience
    • example：The patient will undergo surgery tomorrow if the doctors approve the procedure.

  25. enrol - v.

    • paraphrase：officially join a course or group
    • synonym：register, join
    • example：Students must enrol in the program before the deadline, which is next Friday.

  26. elsewhere - adv.

    • paraphrase：in or to another place
    • synonym：somewhere else, away
    • example：The store was out of stock, so we had to look elsewhere for the ingredients.

  27. panorama - n.

    • paraphrase：wide view of an area
    • synonym：vista, view
    • example：From the hilltop, we saw a panorama of the valley, where rivers cut through forests.

  28. lateral - adj.

    • paraphrase：related to the side
    • synonym：sideways, side
    • example：The car suffered lateral damage when it was hit from the right side.

  29. shore - n.

    • paraphrase：land along the edge of a sea/lake
    • synonym：coast, beach
    • example：We walked along the shore, where waves crashed against the rocks loudly.

  30. frequent - adj./v.

    • paraphrase：① (adj.) happening often; ② (v.) visit often
    • synonym：① regular; ② visit
    • example：He frequents the café downtown because the coffee there is exceptionally good.

  31. chart - n./v.

    • paraphrase：① (n.) visual data display; ② (v.) map or plan
    • synonym：① graph; ② map
    • example：The chart showed sales trends, which helped the team adjust their strategy.

  32. republican - adj./n.

    • paraphrase：supporting a republic system
    • synonym：democratic (context-dependent)
    • example：The republican candidate promised tax reforms, which appealed to middle-class voters.

  33. tactic - n.

    • paraphrase：planned action to achieve a goal
    • synonym：strategy, approach
    • example：Their negotiation tactic was to stay silent until the other side made an offer.

  34. aside - adv./n.

    • paraphrase：① to one side; ② (n.) remark in a play
    • synonym：① apart; ② comment
    • example：She pushed her worries aside and focused on finishing the project before noon.

  35. span - n./v.

    • paraphrase：length of time or space
    • synonym：duration, extend
    • example：The bridge’s span was impressive, stretching over a mile across the river.

  36. ritual - n./adj.

    • paraphrase：ceremony or routine
    • synonym：ceremony, tradition
    • example：Morning coffee became a ritual for him, even when he was traveling.

  37. stock - n./v.

    • paraphrase：① supply of goods; ② (v.) keep goods available
    • synonym：① inventory; ② supply
    • example：The store ran out of stock for the new phone, which disappointed many customers.

  38. incur - v.

    • paraphrase：experience something unpleasant (e.g., debt)
    • synonym：suffer, acquire
    • example：Delaying repairs will incur higher costs later if the damage worsens.

  39. commission - n./v.

    • paraphrase：① (n.) fee or official task; ② (v.) hire someone
    • synonym：① fee; ② hire
    • example：The artist received a commission to paint a mural for the city hall.

  40. forth - adv.

    • paraphrase：forward in time or place
    • synonym：forward, onward
    • example：He paced back and forth in the room while waiting for the test results.

  41. latter - adj.

    • paraphrase：second of two things mentioned
    • synonym：second, last
    • example：Between tea and coffee, she preferred the latter because of its stronger flavor.

  42. organic - adj.

    • paraphrase：natural, without artificial chemicals
    • synonym：natural, pure
    • example：Organic vegetables cost more, but they are grown without synthetic pesticides.

  43. revive - v.

    • paraphrase：bring back to life or consciousness
    • synonym：restore, renew
    • example：The rain revived the dying plants, which had wilted during the drought.

  44. campus - n.

    • paraphrase：university grounds
    • synonym：school grounds, site
    • example：The new library on campus has study rooms where students can work in groups.

  45. conversely - adv.

    • paraphrase：in an opposite way
    • synonym：oppositely, inversely
    • example：Some people love spicy food; conversely, others avoid it completely.

  46. zone - n.

    • paraphrase：area with specific features or rules
    • synonym：area, region
    • example：The factory was built in an industrial zone, where residential buildings are banned.

  47. avail - n./v.

    • paraphrase：use or benefit
    • synonym：use, help
    • example：He tried to fix the leak himself, but his efforts were to no avail.

  48. precaution - n.

    • paraphrase：action to prevent harm
    • synonym：safeguard, measure
    • example：Wearing a helmet is a precaution that reduces head injuries during accidents.

  49. surplus - n./adj.

    • paraphrase：extra amount beyond what’s needed
    • synonym：excess, extra
    • example：The farm produced a surplus of wheat, which they sold to neighboring countries.

  50. rhetoric - n.

    • paraphrase：persuasive speech or writing
    • synonym：oratory, persuasion
    • example：Politicians often use rhetoric to sway public opinion, even if their claims are exaggerated.
